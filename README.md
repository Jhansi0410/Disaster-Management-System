The Disaster Management System (DMS) is a database-driven application developed to efficiently manage and coordinate activities during natural and man-made disasters such as floods, earthquakes, cyclones, and fires. The system serves as a centralized platform that helps authorities handle disaster-related information in an organized and effective manner.

This project focuses on improving the response time and coordination between different entities involved in disaster management, including administrators, rescue teams, and affected users. The system allows administrators to record disaster details, monitor affected locations, assign rescue teams, and track ongoing rescue operations.

Users can register in the system and raise requests for help such as food, shelter, and medical assistance. These requests are stored and managed efficiently, enabling quick response from the authorities. The system also maintains detailed records of victims, including their condition and rescue status, which helps in proper monitoring and support.

Additionally, the system manages essential resources like food supplies, water, and medical kits. By linking resources to specific locations, it ensures that aid is distributed effectively where it is most needed. The use of structured database tables with proper relationships ensures data consistency, integrity, and easy retrieval of information.

Overall, the Disaster Management System enhances disaster preparedness, improves communication, and ensures better resource utilization, ultimately helping to reduce the impact of disasters and save lives.
